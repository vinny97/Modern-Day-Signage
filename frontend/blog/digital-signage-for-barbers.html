Why Every Barber Shop Needs a Digital Display in 2026

By ScreenFizz 25 June 2026 4 min read

Walk into most barber shops and you will find a screen on the wall showing Sky Sports or a music channel. That is fine, customers waiting appreciate having something to watch. But it is also a missed opportunity.

That screen is some of the most valuable real estate in your shop. Every person sitting in your chairs or waiting their turn is looking at it. Here is how to make it work harder for your business.

Show your full price list

A lot of barbers still rely on a printed price list stuck to the wall or a handwritten board near the door. These get ignored, go out of date, and do not do justice to the services you offer.

A digital display gives you a clean, professional price list that is easy to read from the chair. You can show your full range of services, haircuts, beard trims, fades, kids cuts, treatments, with prices that you can update in minutes whenever they change.

No more explaining prices verbally. No more crossed-out numbers on a laminated sheet. Customers know what they are paying before they sit down, which means fewer awkward conversations and faster checkout.

Promote services customers do not know you offer

Most customers come in for one thing, a haircut, and leave without knowing you also do beard sculpting, hot towel shaves, scalp treatments or kids cuts. They find out months later, often from another barber.

A screen in your waiting area is the perfect place to show those services with a short description and a photo. Customers have time to sit, look, and think. Many will ask about something they have just seen, or book it on their next visit.

This is one of the easiest ways to increase your average revenue per customer without spending anything on advertising.

Fill your quiet slots with targeted promotions

Every barber shop has slow periods, Monday mornings, early weekday afternoons, the week after payday. A digital display lets you run targeted promotions during those times.

Something as simple as "Tuesday afternoon, walk-ins welcome, no wait" or a discounted rate for off-peak bookings can shift customers from your busiest periods to your quietest ones. Over time that smooths out your revenue and reduces stress on your team during the rush.

You can update these promotions via WhatsApp in under a minute, so there is no friction in changing what your screen shows week to week.

Look more professional and build your brand

First impressions matter. A barber shop with a professionally branded display, your logo, your colour scheme, your services laid out cleanly, looks more established and trustworthy than one with a consumer TV showing daytime television.

It signals that you take your business seriously. That matters to customers choosing between two barbers on the same street. It also makes a difference in photos, if a customer shares something to Instagram from your chair, what is in the background?

Showcase your work

A rotating gallery of your best cuts is one of the most effective things you can put on a barber shop screen. Customers waiting in your chairs are already thinking about what style they want. Showing them examples of your work, real results from your shop, builds confidence and helps them communicate what they are looking for.

It also acts as a subtle portfolio. A new customer who sees fifteen strong examples of your craft before sitting down is far more likely to trust you with something different or more involved than a standard trim.

What you actually need

You do not need an expensive setup. A single screen behind the counter or in the waiting area is enough to start. The key is having content that is professionally designed and easy to update, not a static image you uploaded once and forgot about.

With a managed service like ScreenFizz, the content is created for you and updated whenever you need. You send a WhatsApp message, "add hot towel shave for £15", and it appears on your screen the same day. No design software, no technical knowledge required.

The screen pays for itself quickly. If one extra beard trim or treatment per day comes from a customer seeing it on your display, the monthly cost is covered within the first week.
