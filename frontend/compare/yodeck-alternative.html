ScreenFizz vs Yodeck

Yodeck is digital signage software, you buy a subscription, set it up yourself, and manage it yourself. ScreenFizz is a fully managed service. We install the screen, configure everything, and keep it running. You update content by sending a WhatsApp message.

The key difference

Yodeck is a good product, but it is still software. That means you need to source hardware, configure the player, design your content, and log in to make updates whenever anything changes. For a business owner, that is work on top of your actual work.

ScreenFizz works differently. You tell us what you need, menu board, window display, promotions, and we handle the rest. One flat monthly fee covers the software, management, content updates, and screen monitoring. If you want something changed on your screen, you send us a WhatsApp message and it is done the same day.

When Yodeck makes sense

Yodeck is a reasonable choice if you have someone in-house who is comfortable managing software, you want to update your own screens, and you do not need installation support. It is a cloud platform that works across a range of hardware.

When ScreenFizz is the better fit

  • You want it done for you. Most small business owners do not have time to learn signage software. ScreenFizz handles setup, content, and maintenance, you just run your business.
  • You need screens installed. We arrange professional TV installation across the UK. Yodeck does not offer this.
  • You want to update via WhatsApp. No logins, no design software. Send us a message and your screen is updated the same day.
  • You want one flat monthly cost. Everything is included, software, management, monitoring, updates.

Pricing comparison

Yodeck starts at around £8 per screen per month for the software. You still need to source hardware, set it up, and manage content yourself, or pay someone to do it. Once you factor in the time cost, the real price is higher.

ScreenFizz starts at £15/month per screen, which includes software, content management, and ongoing support. The £60 one-time player covers the hardware.
