Self-Hosted vs Managed Digital Signage: Which Is Right for Your Business?

By ScreenFizz June 2026 6 min read

There are two ways to run digital signage: self-hosted (you run the software on your own server or device) and managed (a service handles everything for you). Here is an honest comparison of both approaches so you can choose the right fit for your situation.

What is self-hosted digital signage?

Self-hosted signage means you run your own signage software on your own infrastructure, either on a server you control or directly on the player device. You are responsible for setup, configuration, updates, and maintenance.

This approach gives you maximum control and, in some cases, lower ongoing costs. But it requires technical resource to do it well.

What is managed digital signage?

A managed service like ScreenFizz handles the full stack for you, hardware, software, installation, content, and ongoing maintenance. You communicate what you need (usually via WhatsApp or email), and the service team handles execution. You never touch any software or settings.

When self-hosted makes sense

Self-hosted signage is the right choice in a narrow set of circumstances:

  • You have in-house IT capacity. Schools, corporate environments, and organisations with dedicated IT staff who want full control over their infrastructure.
  • Data sovereignty requirements. Healthcare, government, or financial services environments where content cannot leave your network.
  • Large deployments with existing technical resource. If you are managing 50+ screens with a team that can maintain them, self-hosting can reduce per-screen costs at scale.
  • Air-gapped or offline environments. Locations with no internet connection where cloud management is not possible.

When managed makes sense

For most independent businesses in the UK, managed is the better choice:

  • You do not have technical staff. Running signage software on a server requires Linux knowledge, security maintenance, and troubleshooting capacity. If you do not have this in-house, self-hosting creates ongoing risk.
  • Your time is better spent on your business. A cafe owner should be focused on customers, not on why the signage server is unreachable at 7am.
  • You want consistent, professional-looking content. Managed services typically include content design and management, so your screens always look professional without you needing to create anything.
  • You need support when something goes wrong. With a managed service, problems are someone else's problem. With self-hosted, they are yours.

The real cost of self-hosted signage

Self-hosted signage often looks cheaper on paper, no monthly service fee, just server costs. But the full cost includes:

  • Initial setup time (several hours to a full day)
  • Content creation and management (ongoing)
  • Server or hosting costs
  • Security updates and software maintenance
  • Time spent troubleshooting issues

When you factor in the time cost, even at minimum wage, a managed service usually works out cheaper for small businesses, and significantly less stressful.
